Year in Review: My Journey as a Developer

At the start of this year, I felt unsure of myself and questioned whether I even belonged in this field. Everything — from basic HTML and CSS to JavaScript logic — felt overwhelming and unfamiliar. I often found myself second-guessing my work and struggling to keep up.

But over time, and through consistent effort, things started to make more sense. I became more comfortable with the tools, frameworks, and workflows we’ve been using. I’ve built a few projects I’m proud of and learned how to break down problems, ask better questions, and keep going even when things don’t work right away.

I’m still aware that I have a lot to learn — especially when it comes to debugging, managing complex code, and working with APIs. But now, instead of feeling lost, I feel motivated. I’m more confident in my ability to figure things out, and I’m excited to keep growing as a frontend developer.
